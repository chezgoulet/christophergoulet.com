01 — THE STORY

Une histoire de famille

My great-grandfather, Wilfrid Goulet, was one of nearly a million Québécois who came to New England during the Grande Hémorragie — the great migration that built our mill towns and quietly scattered a culture across the border.

For generations, Canada's citizenship laws stripped that nationality from Franco-American women who married Americans — and from their descendants. The Bjorkquist decision and Bill C-3 ended that.

I was among the first to have my citizenship restored under the new law. In French they say "les Canadiens retrouvés" — the found Canadians. It's an emotional road as much as a legal one. I help others walk it.

Bienvenue chez vous.
