# From Family Bakery to Worker Co-op — How Covered Bridge Cookies Changed Everything

**A case study in cooperative conversion, small business resilience, and the technology that made it possible.**

---

## The Setup

Covered Bridge Cookies is a thirty-year-old family bakery in Windsor, Vermont. Four recipes — Gingersnaps, Hermits, Maple Shortbread, and a secret one we don't talk about. Real ingredients, nothing you can't pronounce. For thirty years, it was a family business: recipes passed down, a modest wholesale operation, a loyal local following.

By 2021, the question wasn't whether the business would survive — it was who it would belong to next.

## The Problem

Family businesses face a structural cliff. The founder retires. Who takes over? Sell to a stranger who'll change the recipes? Shut it down? Keep running it solo until burnout?

We chose a third path: **worker ownership**.

But converting a thirty-year-old bakery to a worker co-op isn't a switch you flip. It required:

- **Legal restructuring** — forming a cooperative corporation, transferring assets, rewriting governance
- **Financial restructuring** — revenue needed to support four owners at living wage, not one
- **Cultural restructuring** — the hardest part. Real collaboration — sharing power genuinely — is hard every single day
- **Operational systems** — inventory, ordering, scheduling, compliance. The boring stuff that makes or breaks a small business

## How Technology Fit In

This is where "I make cookies and I build systems" stops being a tagline.

The operational systems that run a co-op are the same shape as the systems that run infrastructure. Recipes → source code. Production line → pipeline. Package → build. Customer → deploy.

I needed technology that was:

- **Owned, not rented** — no monthly per-seat SaaS subscriptions bleeding the margin
- **Documented for one person to run** — no vendor lock-in, no single point of failure
- **Simple enough that no full-time admin was needed** — the bakers shouldn't need IT support to do their jobs

The same principles apply whether you're running a cookie bakery or a private AI cluster.

## The Results

In three years:

- **3× revenue growth**
- **100% worker-owned** — four owners, living wage, good conditions
- **30-year legacy kept** — the recipes, the name, the community presence
- **Bilingual operation** — English and French, always

The legacy stays with the people who do the work.

## What It Taught Me

Three things I now bring to every client engagement:

**1. Governance is infrastructure.** How decisions get made shapes everything downstream. A co-op's governance documents aren't paperwork — they're the operating system of the business.

**2. The technology must match the values.** If you believe in ownership, your tools should reflect that. Open source where possible. Self-hosted where practical. Transparent, documented, auditable.

**3. Resilience is a choice you make every morning.** Co-op conversion isn't a one-time event. It's a daily practice of choosing collaboration over convenience, shared power over efficiency.
